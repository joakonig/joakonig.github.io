Thesis: Gender‑Differentiated Impact of PM2.5 Exposure

Author: Joaquín Vargas Salamanca

Institution: Universidad de Chile

Year: 2020

Abstract

This thesis investigates the gender‑differentiated impact of PM2.5 air pollution on respiratory and cardiovascular mortality rates across Latin America. The analysis applies econometric and statistical models to estimate health outcomes associated with exposure to PM2.5 concentrations, controlling for socioeconomic and demographic factors.

The findings highlight the importance of considering gender differences in public health policy design, particularly in contexts with substantial environmental inequality.
